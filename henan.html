About Henan

A land where ancient civilization meets modern vitality

Geography & Location

Henan Province is situated in the central part of the People's Republic of China, lying between latitude 31°23' and 36°22' north, and longitude 110°21' and 116°39' east. The province covers a total area of 167,000 square kilometers, bordered by Anhui and Shandong to the east, Hebei and Shanxi to the north, Shaanxi to the west, and Hubei to the south.

The terrain of Henan is diverse, with mountains occupying approximately 44.3% of the total area, primarily in the west and northwest. The province is traversed by the Yellow River, the Qinling-Huaihe Line (the natural boundary between northern and southern China), and features vast fertile plains across its central region.

Climate & Environment

Henan has a warm temperate continental monsoon climate with four distinct seasons. Spring is warm and windy, summer is hot and rainy, autumn is cool and pleasant, and winter is cold and dry. The average annual temperature ranges from 12°C to 16°C, with annual precipitation between 500 and 900 millimeters.

The province is home to rich biodiversity, including numerous nature reserves such as the Funiu Mountain Nature Reserve, which protects rare species like the giant panda, golden monkey, and Chinese ibis. The diverse ecosystems support a wide variety of flora and fauna.

Demographics & Economy

With a population exceeding 99 million, Henan is the third most populous province in China. The capital city, Zhengzhou, serves as a major national transportation hub and is one of China's largest cities. The province has 17 prefecture-level cities under its administration.

Henan is one of China's most important agricultural regions, known as the "Granary of the Central Plains." It is the country's largest producer of wheat, and a major producer of corn, cotton, and other crops. The province is also rapidly developing its industrial and technology sectors.

Culture & Heritage

Henan is widely recognized as the birthplace of Chinese civilization. It was the political, economic, and cultural center of China for over 3,000 years, serving as the capital for more than 20 dynasties. The province is home to four of China's eight ancient capitals: Luoyang, Kaifeng, Anyang, and Zhengzhou.

The province's rich cultural heritage includes the oracle bone inscriptions (the earliest known Chinese writing), the origins of Chinese calligraphy, traditional opera (Yuju Opera), martial arts (Shaolin Kung Fu), and countless historical sites that continue to attract scholars and visitors from around the world.

Fast Facts

Provincial Capital

Zhengzhou, a major national transportation hub and one of China's eight ancient capitals with a history of over 3,000 years.

Province Abbreviation

"Yu" is the official abbreviation, derived from the ancient Yu State that existed in this region during the Zhou Dynasty.

Four Ancient Capitals

Luoyang, Kaifeng, Anyang, and Zhengzhou each served as capitals for multiple dynasties throughout Chinese history.

Birthplace of Writing

Oracle bone inscriptions discovered in Anyang represent the earliest known form of Chinese characters, dating back 3,300 years.

Shaolin Kung Fu

Originating from the Shaolin Temple on Mount Song, this legendary martial art has influenced fighting styles worldwide.

Economic Hub

Henan's GDP ranks among the top five in China, with strengths in agriculture, manufacturing, and emerging technology industries.
